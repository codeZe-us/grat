Imagine you have built a seamless USDC off-ramp for a small merchant in Lagos, Nigeria. The user has downloaded your app and received their first digital payment. They are ready to withdraw to their local bank, but the transaction fails. They do not have XLM to pay for the network fee. You now have to explain what a "native asset" is and why they need to buy a second currency just to move the first one. This friction kills adoption before it even begins.

Grat solves this by allowing your application to pay the network fees for your users. By leveraging native Stellar fee-bumps, you can provide a zero-gas experience that feels like a modern fintech app, not a complex blockchain protocol.

### How it works in 3 steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build a Transaction">
    Your application builds a standard Stellar or Soroban transaction as usual. Your user signs it to authorize the intent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to Grat">
    Instead of submitting to the network, your app sends the signed transaction to the Grat relay via the SDK or REST API.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sponsorship and Submission">
    Grat wraps your transaction in a fee-bump envelope, pays the fee using its own channel accounts, and submits it to the network.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### What is available today

Grat is currently optimized for the Stellar Testnet while mainnet features are being finalized.

* **Classic Payments**: Full support for XLM and Asset transfers.
* **Soroban Support**: Native sponsorship for smart contract invocations and resource fees.
* **TypeScript SDK**: A resilient, type-safe client with automatic retries.
* **REST API**: Standard endpoints for integration with any programming language.

<Info>
Mainnet support, API key management, and the policy engine are currently in active development and will ship in Phase 2 and 3.
</Info>

### The Apache 2.0 Advantage

We believe that core infrastructure should be as permissive as possible. Unlike alternative solutions that use AGPL licenses, Grat uses the Apache 2.0 license. This allows you to integrate, modify, and distribute Grat as part of your commercial application without being forced to open-source your proprietary business logic.
